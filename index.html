‚ú® About Navratri ‚ú®

Navratri is one of the most important Hindu festivals, dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga and her nine forms. The word "Navratri" means "nine nights," during which devotees honor and celebrate the divine feminine power.

This festival symbolizes the victory of good over evil, as Goddess Durga defeated the demon Mahishasura after a fierce battle. Each day of Navratri is dedicated to a different form of the goddess, from Shailaputri on the first day to Siddhidatri on the ninth.

Devotees observe fasting, chant mantras, and participate in cultural activities such as Garba and Dandiya. The nine days are also associated with specific colors, symbolizing purity, energy, and devotion.

Across India, Navratri is celebrated uniquely: Garba in Gujarat, Durga Puja in West Bengal, and Ramleela in North India. Despite regional variations, the essence remains the same‚Äîdevotion to the Divine Mother.

Navratri reminds us that righteousness and faith always triumph over negativity. The nine forms of Goddess Durga bless devotees with protection, prosperity, and spiritual progress.
