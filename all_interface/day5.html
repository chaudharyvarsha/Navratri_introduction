About Goddess Skandamata

Skandamata is the fifth form of Goddess Durga, worshipped on the fifth day of Navratri. She is the mother of Lord Kartikeya (also known as Skanda), hence the name Skandamata. She is depicted riding a lion, carrying Kartikeya on her lap.

She has four arms ‚Äî two holding lotuses, one carrying her son, and the fourth hand granting blessings. She symbolizes motherly love, compassion, and protection. Devotees believe that worshipping her brings wisdom, prosperity, and salvation. She governs the Vishuddha (throat) chakra, representing purity of expression.
