Day 2: Brahmacharini

About Goddess Brahmacharini

Brahmacharini is the second form of Goddess Durga, worshipped on the second day of Navratri. She represents devotion, penance, and meditation. She holds a rosary in one hand and a water pot (kamandalu) in the other.

She performed severe penance for thousands of years to obtain Lord Shiva as her husband. She is a symbol of patience, strength, and self-control. Worshippers seek her blessings for wisdom, peace, and liberation. In yoga, she governs the Swadhisthana (sacral) chakra.
