Day 3: Chandraghanta

About Goddess Chandraghanta

Chandraghanta is worshipped on the third day of Navratri. She is named for the crescent moon (chandra) shaped like a bell (ghanta) on her forehead. She rides a tiger, symbolizing bravery and fearlessness.

She has ten arms, holding weapons to fight demons, and blesses her devotees with courage and protection. Her bell-shaped moon signifies victory over evil. She is believed to open the Manipura (solar plexus) chakra, spreading divine energy and removing negativity.
